    About The Park

    In the fall of 2009, while playing at Amazon Dog Park in Eugene, Oregon, Lola, an 11 pound dog (shown above), was attacked from behind by a large dog. In front of Lola's owner, her four year old daughter and many others, Lola was mauled to death.

    For Lola's owner, the sorrow and loss were almost overwhelming. She wanted to make certain that no small dog ever suffered such a fate at a dog park again.

    She turned her grief into action by contacting the City's parks department, the Mayor, and the City Council asking if Eugene could have a dog park just for small dogs. Through her online petition, posters, flyers and the media, word spread. Countless stories came forward of small dogs injured by larger dogs. As a result her owner Renee vowed to bring into creation our small dog park.

    Dog Park Tips

    • Tip #1

      If you take the time to be an informed dog owner, you will be able to judge for yourself if the situation you and your dog are in is a good situation – so, have fun at the dog park - but keep an eye on your dog at all times.

    • Tip #2

      Be sure to take your dog’s temperament into consideration and don’t assume s/he’s having a good time – watch your dog’s demeanor and make an informed judgment about how happy she is to be there.

    • Tip #3

      Please remove your dog from the park if they become agressive towards the other dogs.
